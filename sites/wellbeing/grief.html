Grief

Grief comes in waves. These tools help you stay afloat on rough days, create gentle rituals, and plan for dates when feelings surge.

The Waves Model

It can feel better, then worse, then better again.

  • Big wave: lower expectations, ask for help, basics only (food, water, rest).
  • Small wave: short walk, one message to someone you trust, light task.

Not Linear

A surge later doesn’t mean you’re back at the start.

Often linked to dates, places, music, smells, or tiredness. Note the trigger; plan one small comfort next time.

Plan key dates

2-Minute Anchor

  1. Exhale fully
  2. Inhale 4 • Hold 4 • Exhale 4 • Hold 4
  3. Repeat 4–6 cycles

Pair with a brief outdoor pause or watering a plant.

Rituals That Help

  • Grow a pot in their honour; note first leaves/flowers.
  • Memory notebook: a phrase, a story, a recipe.
  • A weekly walk or cup of tea at a set time; battery candle.

Your Grief Toolbox

Pick 2–3 small, repeatable things.

  • Walk for 10 minutes, or water plants slowly
  • Write 5 lines: what you miss + one gratitude
  • Breathing tool + drink of water

Let One Person In

Message a friend: “Today is heavy. A short call or a walk would help.”

Anniversaries & Dates

  • Book something light; invite a check-in
  • Have an “opt-out” message ready
  • Keep the day simple; rest is allowed

Physiology First

  • Hydration before caffeine
  • Simple meals (protein + carbs + fruit/veg)
  • Wind-down window at night

When to Seek Help

  • Persistent inability to function
  • Intrusive thoughts or urges to self-harm
  • Traumatic or sudden loss, or complicated grief

Anticipatory Grief

  • Name it; it’s normal
  • Shorten plans; accept waves
  • Capture stories, photos, favourite sayings

Supporting Children

  • Use clear words (“died”), short sentences
  • Answer questions again later
  • Keep routines; add small memorials

Sudden Loss

  • Basics only: water, food, rest
  • Repeat info; write decisions down
  • Accept practical help

Helpful Links (UK)

  • Winston’s Wish — supporting bereaved children: winstonswish.org
  • Sudden — support after sudden bereavement: sudden.org
