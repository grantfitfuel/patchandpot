Neurodiversity

Practical, judgment-free tools for ADHD, autism, dyslexia, dyspraxia and more. Tune your environment, protect energy, and make tasks doable on real-life days.

Sensory Dials

You can turn dials — not “fix” yourself.

  • Sound: headphones, brown noise, soft earplugs.
  • Light: warm lamp, cap/hood, screen tint.
  • Touch: seams/tags off, soft layer, fidget.
  • Movement: mini-walks, stretch timer, rocking.
  • Smell: neutral soap, window crack, fresh herb.

Masking & Burnout

Masking (hiding traits to fit) costs energy. Over time: fatigue, shutdown, anxiety.

  • Schedule decompression after social/work blocks.
  • One “self” place daily (clothes, music, stims).
  • Reduce masking with one safe person or activity.

Energy (Spoons)

  • Budget spoons: must-do vs nice-to-do.
  • Pair drains with buffers (call → walk → snack).
  • Protect one empty slot daily for repairs.

Task Slicing

Make it finishable.

  • 1. Put pot on the counter
  • 2. Fill jug
  • 3. Water two plants (done!)

Body Double

Work near someone (or on a call) for gentle accountability.

Try a 10-minute double

Cues > Willpower

  • Lay out meds + water where you look in the morning.
  • One tray by the door: keys, card, mask, cap.
  • Timers you can hear from the next room.

Home

  • Clear “runway” surfaces (one empty spot per room).
  • Duplicate essentials (scissors, chargers) in zones.
  • Labels and transparent boxes > mystery baskets.

Work/Study

  • Noise control, flexible breaks, written instructions.
  • One-task briefs, visual checklists, deadline reminders.
  • Camera-off / chat-first options for meetings.

Know Your Rights

Ask about reasonable adjustments. Keep notes of requests and outcomes.
