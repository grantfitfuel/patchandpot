Cut Flowers in Pots

Small containers, repeat cutting, big mood lift — even on a windowsill.

Good Pot Flowers

  • Sweet peas (dwarf), calendula, dwarf cosmos, violas.
  • Nasturtiums — edible and cheerful.
  • Chives and basil flowers for pollinators.

Planting

  • Peat-free compost with added slow-release feed.
  • Support sweet peas; deadhead for repeat blooms.
  • Water deeply; don’t let them bake dry in summer.

Cut & Enjoy

  • Cut early morning; condition in water 30 minutes.
  • Change vase water often; recut stems every 2–3 days.
  • Mix foliage (herbs) for scent and texture.
