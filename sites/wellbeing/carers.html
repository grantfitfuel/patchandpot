Carer Support

Caring can be heavy, even when it’s done with love. Use these small, realistic tools to reduce overload, steady your day, and get the right help in place.

90-Second Reset

  • Water + 4 rounds of box breathing (4-4-4-4)
  • Open a window; look at something green
  • One small task only (e.g., put on a load, reply to one message)

Anchor Your Day

Tiny repeatable cues keep you steadier.

  • AM: drink, meds, 2-minute calm
  • Midday: food + 5-minute walk/stretch
  • PM: wind-down window; same bedtime

Boundaries (Kind & Firm)

“I can do X today; I can’t do Y. Let’s plan Y for tomorrow or ask for help.”

Scripts help when you’re tired — write two you’ll actually use.

The Clipboard

  • Current meds, doses, allergies
  • Today’s appointments and travel plan
  • Top 3 to-dos only; everything else is optional

Forms & Finances

  • Benefits and carer’s allowance eligibility
  • Power of attorney / consent to share
  • Keep scans in a cloud folder + one paper set
Find advice services

Backup & Rota

List two people for each: pharmacy run, hospital lift, sit-with-them hour.

People want to help — make it simple and specific.

Sleep Is a Tool

  • Protect a 45–60 min wind-down
  • Dim screens; prep clothes/meds for morning
  • Short naps (20–30 min) beat none

Simple Food

  • Protein + carb + fruit/veg each meal
  • Keep 3 “default” meals you can make tired
  • Keep a water bottle near you

Move in 5

Two 5-minute walks or stretches are enough to lift energy and mood.
