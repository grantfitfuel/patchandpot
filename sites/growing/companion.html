Companion Planting

Pair crops that support each other — better flavour, more pollinators, fewer pests. Tuned for **containers** and small spaces.

Great Combos

  • Tomato + Basil — flavour boost, similar water/light.
  • Chilli + Marigold (tagetes) — flowers pull pollinators, deter some pests.
  • Kale + Chives — chives help deter aphids; compact roots.
  • Carrot + Spring onion — scent confuses carrot fly; both shallow-rooted.
  • Strawberry + Thyme — thyme’s flowers bring bees; thyme likes same drainage.

Things to Avoid

  • Overcrowding — poor airflow = mildew & pests.
  • Mismatched thirst — don’t pot rosemary with basil.
  • Deep + shallow roots in tiny pots — they’ll compete badly.
  • Note: avoid dill + carrots in small containers (carrot fly risk on brushing foliage).

Pollinator Boost

  • Edible flowers: nasturtiums, calendula, violas.
  • Let a few herbs flower: basil, chives, oregano.
  • Keep pesticide-free; use sticky cards / hand-control first.

Container Pairings (by pot size)

PotPlant TogetherWhy it worksSkip this
Window box (5–7 L) Parsley + Chives + Viola Shallow roots, frequent snips, bee-friendly flowers. Tomato (too thirsty, top heavy)
10–12 L Chilli + Basil Warmth lovers; basil shades soil, reduces evaporation. Mint (invasive; pot it alone)
20 L Tomato (bush) + Basil + Marigold Classic trio: pollinators + flavour + pest distraction. Potato (disease crossover, too big)
40 L tub Courgette + Nasturtiums (edge) Nasturtiums trail & draw aphids away; big leaves shade soil. Another courgette (overcrowds fast)

Rule of thumb: if both plants like the same light and watering rhythm, they’re likely companions in a pot.
