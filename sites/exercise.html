Move gently

Exercise

Short, realistic sessions that fit around life and growing. You don’t need a gym — just your body, a bit of space, and steady progress.

Movement is one of the simplest ways to support both body and mind. Regular, manageable sessions help build strength, ease stiffness, boost circulation, and settle the nervous system. These plans are intentionally short and flexible — perfect between gardening tasks, in the kitchen, or in the living room.

If you’re brand new to exercise, start with the warm-up or one bodyweight round, and build gradually. If you’re further along, add intensity by increasing reps, using light weights, or combining sessions. Always move at your own pace and respect how your body feels on the day.

5-Minute Warm-up

Repeat once or twice to ease into movement.

  1. Neck rolls × 3 each way — slow and gentle, no strain.
  2. Shoulder circles × 10 — open the chest and upper back.
  3. Hip hinges × 10 — wake up your posterior chain (hips, hamstrings, lower back).
  4. Air squats × 10 — keep heels down, back tall.
  5. Calf raises × 12 — lift slowly, pause at the top.

Warm-ups help your muscles, joints, and lungs prepare for movement — and lower the risk of injury.

Bodyweight Basics

2–3 rounds · rest 45–60 s between moves.

  • Squat × 12 — push through your heels, keep knees tracking over toes.
  • Incline press-up × 10 — hands on a worktop or table, chest lowers between hands.
  • Split squat × 10 each side — steady hold on a wall or chair if needed.
  • Dead bug or plank × 20–30 s — focus on controlled breathing and bracing.

A solid foundation for mobility, balance, and strength — no equipment required. Adjust the reps or pace to match your comfort level.

Dumbbell Pair

A light pair is plenty. 2–3 rounds with steady breathing.

  • Goblet squat × 12 — hold one dumbbell at your chest, drive up from heels.
  • Floor press × 10 — lie on your back, elbows close, press weights upwards.
  • Bent row × 10 — hinge at the hips, squeeze shoulder blades as you pull.
  • Seated curl × 10 — elbows tucked, control the descent.

Great for gently building strength, supporting bone density, and improving daily movement capacity.

Cool Down (2–3 mins)

Slow nasal breathing · calf/quad/hamstring stretch 20–30 s each side.

  • Gentle standing hamstring stretch
  • Quad stretch with support (hold wall or chair)
  • Seated or standing calf stretch
  • Shoulder and chest opening

Cool-down helps your heart rate settle and reduces post-exercise soreness.

Progression

Start small and build. Try adding:

  • +1 rep per week on each exercise
  • One extra round of the same set
  • Heavier dumbbells when ready
  • A longer plank or slower tempo

Progress doesn’t need to be huge. Tiny, regular steps add up over months.

Mindful Movement

Movement can be a reset. Some days you’ll have energy, some days you won’t — that’s okay. Even 3 minutes of light stretching, a slow walk, or deep breathing still counts.
