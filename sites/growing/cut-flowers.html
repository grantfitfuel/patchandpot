Cut Flowers in Small Spaces

From window boxes to recycled crates — learn how to grow colour, scent and joy all season long.

Mindful Growing

Flowers aren’t just decoration: they shift mood, give you mindful pauses, and mark the rhythm of the seasons. Whether you cut a single stem for the kitchen or fill a jug for the table, every bloom is a small act of care. Notice the textures, colours and scents as you grow — tending flowers is as restorative as enjoying them indoors.

Containers Beyond Pots

  • Crates & fruit boxes: line with hessian or compost sacks, drill drainage holes, plant with a shallow-root mix like calendula + violas.
  • Galvanised buckets: drill holes, fill with cosmos or snapdragons for a cottage look.
  • Reclaimed drawers & trunks: line with polythene pierced for drainage; great for mixed colour schemes.
  • Barrels & half barrels: large enough for dahlias, gladioli or a whole lasagne of bulbs.
  • Window boxes: choose 20 cm depth minimum; water daily in summer.
  • Tin cans & jars (short-lived): direct sow dwarf calendula or nasturtiums for a quick colour hit.

If it holds compost and drains, it can hold flowers. Always drill drainage holes and raise containers on pot feet or bricks.

Soil Mix & Feeding

Flower mix

  • 60% peat-free compost
  • 25–30% wood fibre / fine bark
  • 10–15% grit or perlite
  • Slow release fertiliser for 10–12 weeks

Feeding plan

  • 0–4 weeks: water only.
  • 5+ weeks: weekly liquid feed, half strength.
  • Heavy feeders: dahlias, sunflowers, zinnias need feeding twice a week in heat.

Scratch in 1–2 cm fresh compost mid-season for tired pots.

Hardy & Half-Hardy Choices

Hardy annuals

  • Calendula, cornflower, nigella, scabious, larkspur, ammi, dill.
  • Autumn sow in mild areas, spring in cooler regions. Protect with fleece in breezy spots.

Half-hardy annuals

  • Cosmos, rudbeckia, sunflower, snapdragon, amaranthus, zinnia, celosia.
  • Sow late Mar–Apr; plant out late May–Jun (later in the north).

Bulbs, Corms & Tubers

  • Spring: tulips (plant in Nov for tall stems), narcissus, alliums.
  • Summer: lilies (stake), gladioli (plant in succession), dahlias (1 per 40 L).

Bulb Lasagnes (Layered Pots)

Layer bulbs by depth to give flowers in sequence — crocus in February, narcissus in March, tulips in April–May, and even annuals afterwards.

How to

  1. Choose a deep pot (30–40 cm). Add drainage.
  2. Base: tulips/alliums (20–25 cm deep).
  3. Middle: narcissus, hyacinth (12–15 cm).
  4. Top: crocus, muscari, dwarf iris (5–8 cm).
  5. Cover each layer with compost, water in, and leave outside to root.

Colour recipes

  • Calm Whites: crocus ‘Jeanne d’Arc’, narcissus ‘Thalia’, tulip ‘Purissima’.
  • Blue & Gold: muscari, narcissus ‘Tête-à-Tête’, tulip ‘Ballerina’.
  • Bold Jewels: crocus mixed, hyacinth ‘Woodstock’, tulip ‘Queen of Night’.

After tulips fade, tuck in fast annuals (calendula, nigella) in top 5 cm to extend colour into summer.

Year-Round Colour & Planning

  • Spring: bulb lasagnes, primroses, violas.
  • Summer: cosmos, zinnia, sunflowers, dahlias.
  • Autumn: rudbeckia, amaranthus, chrysanthemums.
  • Winter: cyclamen, evergreen foliage, berried shrubs.

Plan a colour “story” — e.g. calming whites & blues, or fiery oranges & reds. Group containers in complementary palettes for impact.

Harvest & Conditioning

  • Cut in early morning. Use clean buckets, strip lower leaves.
  • Stage to cut: cosmos with first petals, snapdragon at colour show, zinnia half open.
  • Re-cut stems under water; rest cool for 2–4 hours. Use preservative or sugar + acid + a drop of bleach.
