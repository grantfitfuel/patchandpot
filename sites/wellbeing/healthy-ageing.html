Healthy Ageing

Ageing well is less about luck and more about systems: movement that protects joints and builds strength, food that feeds body and brain (and the gut microbes that help run both), sleep that repairs, and social ties that keep us going. This page gives you clear explanations, sets & reps, plus inline demo videos so you can learn form safely.

Stretching & Mobility

6–8 minutes daily keeps joints moving and muscles loose.

  • Neck circles, shoulder rolls (8 each)
  • Hip & ankle circles (10 each)
  • Calf stretch (20s each side)

Strength Training

2–3 sessions a week. Start with bodyweight, then progress to light dumbbells.

  • Sit-to-Stand: 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps
  • Wall Push-ups: 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps
  • Hip Hinge: 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps
  • March & Balance: 2 sets × 30–45s

Walking

Walking improves heart, mood, and brain health. Brisk intervals add extra benefit.

Swimming & Water Exercise

Water supports joints and offers natural resistance.

Balance (Tai Chi • Qi Gong • Yoga • Chair Yoga)

Balance training prevents falls and boosts core stability.

Nutrition & the Microbiome

Feed your gut microbes — they repay you with energy, immunity, and brain support.

  • 25–30g fibre/day (beans, oats, veg, fruit)
  • Fermented foods (yoghurt, kefir, kimchi)
  • Healthy fats (olive oil, nuts, oily fish)

Macronutrients

  • Protein: 20–30g per meal (muscle repair)
  • Carbs: whole grains, roots, beans
  • Fats: olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds

Blueberries & Brain Foods

Berries, leafy greens, nuts, and olive oil improve memory and focus.

Hydration

Dehydration can mimic fatigue or confusion. Small sips often are best.

Cognition & Sleep

7–9 hours of quality sleep repairs memory and mood.

Community & Purpose

Group activities (walking, classes, volunteering) build purpose and prevent isolation.

Important Note
