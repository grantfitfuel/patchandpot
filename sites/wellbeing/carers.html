Carer Support

Caring for someone can feel like a privilege, but it often comes with stress, exhaustion, and grief. You deserve help and support as much as the person you care for. This page gives clear explanations, coping strategies, and trusted links to national and regional services in the UK.

1. Why caring is so hard

Carers juggle physical tasks, emotional strain, and constant vigilance. This leads to:

  • Stress overload: Your nervous system stays in “on-call” mode.
  • Anticipatory grief: Watching gradual decline is emotionally draining.
  • Isolation: Friendships and hobbies may fade as caring dominates.

2. Mental health impact

Many carers quietly experience anxiety, depression, or both:

  • Anxiety from “what if?” thoughts and constant risk-scanning.
  • Depression from reduced time for joy, meaning, and rest.
  • Sleep loss, which worsens both conditions.

3. Coping strategies

  • Take micro-breaks — 2 minutes of breathing by a window still helps.
  • Use anchors — small rituals (tea in a favourite mug, music) signal calm.
  • Share tasks — ask family for very specific help.
  • Try reframing — “I’m trapped” → “This moment will pass.”
  • Affirmation: “I’ll do enough today.”

4. Support services (National)

  • Carers UK — advice line, factsheets, peer support forums.
  • Carers Trust — local carer support services across the UK.
  • NHS Carer Support — benefits, respite, and practical help.
  • Mind (for Carers) — mental health advice and self-care guides.
  • Samaritans — free 24/7 listening: 116 123.

5. Regional support

Scotland

  • Carers Scotland — part of Carers UK, tailored Scottish advice.
  • Breathing Space — 0800 83 85 87 (for low mood, anxiety).

England

  • NHS Young Carers Directory — find services in your area.
  • Rethink Mental Illness — advice and support groups for families.

Wales

  • Carers Wales — local advice and support lines.
  • CALL Helpline Wales — emotional support: 0800 132 737.

Northern Ireland

  • Carers NI — practical help and policy updates.
  • Minding Your Head — mental health resources for NI residents.

6. When it feels overwhelming

If you ever feel unsafe with your thoughts, reach out immediately:

  • Samaritans: 116 123
  • Shout: text SHOUT to 85258
  • Breathing Space (Scotland): 0800 83 85 87
  • CALL Helpline (Wales): 0800 132 737
  • Lifeline (NI): 0808 808 8000

You are not alone, and support is always available — day or night.

7. Protecting your own identity

Small steps keep you connected to who you are, beyond being a carer:

  • Keep a hobby alive, even 5 minutes at a time.
  • Stay in touch with a friend — one short call can lift your day.
  • Allow hope — imagine life “after” caring. It isn’t betrayal, it’s preparation.
