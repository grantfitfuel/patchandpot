Cut Flowers in Pots

Buckets of blooms from containers — window boxes, steps, balconies and tiny beds.

Pots & Spacing

Pot sizeTypical plantsCount
3–5 L boxCalendula, cornflower, dill, nigella3–5
10 L tubCosmos dwarf, scabious, snapdragon dwarf3
20 L tubCosmos tall, rudbeckia, dwarf sunflower3
40 L tubSingle dahlia or tall sunflower (stake)1–2
60 L troughMixed annuals with mesh support8–12
  • Stake or add mesh at planting; wind breaks stems.
  • Daily water in heat; use saucers you can remove after a soak.
  • Anchor pots on balconies with straps/bricks; add pot feet for drainage.

Deep pots (30–40 cm) with a wide base are far more wind-safe on balconies and rooftops.

Soil Mix & Feeding

Container mix

  • 60% peat-free compost
  • 30% fine bark / wood fibre
  • 10% perlite or grit
  • Slow-release feed (10–12 weeks)

Feeding

  • Weeks 0–4: water only; keep evenly moist.
  • Weeks 5+: liquid feed weekly at half strength.
  • Dahlias/zinnias: twice weekly in hot spells.

Scratch in 1–2 cm fresh compost mid-season for big tubs.

What to Grow (by hardiness)

Hardy annuals (cool-tolerant)

  • Calendula, cornflower, nigella, ammi, scabious, dill, larkspur.
  • Autumn sow in mild spots; spring sow elsewhere. Harden off with fleece on breezy days.

Half-hardy (need frost-free)

  • Cosmos, snapdragon, rudbeckia, sunflower, amaranthus, zinnia (warmest).
  • Sow late Mar–Apr; plant out late May–Jun (north: later). Pinch for branching, stake early.

Bulbs, Corms, Tubers

  • Spring pots: tulips (Nov plant for stems), narcissus, alliums.
  • Summer pots: lilies (stake), gladioli (stagger plantings), dahlias (1 per 40 L).
  • Drainage is critical: pot feet, soak deeply, then drain off.

Simple Succession

Early

  • Feb–Apr: sow calendula, cornflower, nigella, dill in modules; plant out with mesh.

Main season

  • Apr–Jun: sow cosmos, snapdragon, rudbeckia. Add a fresh 10–20 L pot every 2–3 weeks.

Harvest & Conditioning

  • Cut early morning; clean buckets; strip lower leaves.
  • Stages: snapdragon at colour showing; cosmos at first petals; zinnia half-open.
  • Re-cut under water; rest cool 2–4 hours. Preservative or sugar + acid + tiny bleach.
