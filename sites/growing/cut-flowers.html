Creative Containers (Skip the pots & troughs)

What to turn into planters

  • Woven baskets — line with hessian or a thick paper compost bag; add drainage holes.
  • Crates & wine boxes — staple a liner; great for short-stem fillers.
  • Old metal tins & paint cans — hammer 6–8 holes in the base; edge-safe the rim with tape.
  • Wire shelves or ladders — cable-tie lined baskets to the rungs for a vertical “flower ladder”.
  • Guttering (offcut) — cap ends, drill base holes every 12–15 cm; hang along a sunny railing.
  • Drawers & suitcases — line + drainage; prop lids for wind-breaks.
  • Colanders/steamers — instant drainage; nest a saucer below when watering.

Drainage rule: you can use almost anything if water can escape and the sides don’t collapse. Add a scrap of mesh over big holes.

Anchoring & safety

  • Secure to railings with metal brackets + cable ties (belt & braces).
  • Use brick weights or sandbags inside crates on windy balconies.
  • Keep heavy pieces low (floor or step), light pieces high.
  • If you’re in a flat, stick within balcony load guidance.

DIY self-watering insert (fits baskets/crates)

  • Lay a thin washing-up sponge or capillary mat at the base.
  • Add a short perforated bottle (cap off) as a water well.
  • Backfill with mix; water via the bottle during heatwaves.

Container Mix & Feeding

All-purpose flower mix

  • 60% peat-free compost
  • 25–30% fine bark/wood fibre
  • 10–15% perlite or grit
  • Handful of slow-release fertiliser (10–12 wks)

Lined baskets and drawers dry faster — mix in extra wood fibre to hold moisture.

Feeding rhythm

  • Weeks 0–4: water evenly; no liquid feed.
  • Weeks 5+: weekly liquid feed at half strength.
  • Heavy feeders (dahlia, zinnia, sunflower): twice weekly in heat.

Over-watering is the #1 fail in lined containers. Let the top 2–3 cm dry before watering again.

What to Grow (easy winners in containers)

Hardy annual fillers

  • Calendula (orange/gold) — long season, edible.
  • Cornflower — classic blues; great in guttering runs.
  • Nigella — flowers & sculptural seedpods.
  • Dill & Ammi — airy umbels, pollinator magnets.
  • Scabious — ping-pong blooms that keep coming.

Sow Sept–Oct (mild/city) or Feb–Apr elsewhere. Fleece in cold snaps.

Half-hardy “wow” stems

  • Cosmos — endless flowers; pinch once at 15–20 cm.
  • Snapdragon — staggered heights for longer stems.
  • Rudbeckia — warm tones; pairs well with grasses.
  • Zinnia (warmest sites) — pick often for more.
  • Amaranthus — trailing or upright drama.

Sow late Mar–Apr; plant out late May–Jun (north a little later).

Fragrance & accent

  • Sweet pea (dwarf series) — railing runs + netting.
  • Helichrysum — paper daisies; great dried.
  • Feverfew & achillea — bright filler buttons.
  • Dwarf sunflower — a few giants in crates look epic.
  • Grasses (carex, stipa) — movement & texture.

Bulbs, Corms & Tubers for Small Spaces

  • Spring — tulips (plant Nov for long stems), narcissus, alliums.
  • Summer — lilies (stake in crates), gladioli (stagger plantings), dahlias (one tuber per lined crate).
  • Drainage is everything: raise containers on feet; water deeply, then let excess go.

Simple Succession (UK-tuned)

Early season

  • Feb–Apr: start hardy fillers in modules (indoors or sheltered).
  • Move to baskets/crates with mesh or a few canes.

Main season

  • Apr–Jun: half-hardy stars (cosmos, snapdragons, zinnia for warmest spots).
  • Every 2–3 weeks, add one new container to keep stems rolling.

North/windy coast: delay half-hardy by ~2 weeks; use fleece & wind-break mesh when hardening off.

Harvest & Conditioning (longer-lasting stems)

  • Cut in the morning. Use clean buckets; strip lower leaves.
  • Stage matters: snapdragons at colour-showing; cosmos at first petals; zinnia half-open (do the “wiggle test”).
  • Re-cut under water; rest cool 2–4 hours. Preservative, or a home mix: water + a pinch of sugar + a splash of lemon/lime + a tiny drop of bleach.

Mindful Moments (use while you water)

  • 5-sense scan: name 1 thing you can see, hear, feel, and smell right now among your flowers.
  • Square breath: in 4 · hold 4 · out 4 · hold 4 × 4 rounds while you check leaves.
  • Micro-gratitude: note one tiny change since yesterday (a bud, a new leaf, a visiting bee).
