Your First Pot

A simple, forgiving setup you can finish in 15–20 minutes. Works on a windowsill, doorstep, or balcony. The first pot you grow is the start of something much bigger.

What you need

  • ✓ 25–30 cm pot with a drainage hole
  • ✓ Peat-free multi-purpose compost
  • ✓ Seeds or young plant (basil, parsley, salad leaves)
  • ✓ Small jug or bottle for watering
  • ✓ Label & pencil (optional but helpful)

Step-by-step

  1. Prep the base: Cover the hole with a crocks shard or mesh so compost doesn’t leak.
  2. Fill: Add compost to 2 cm below the rim (space for watering).
  3. Plant:
    • Seedlings: Make a hole, place the plant at the same depth, gently firm around it.
    • Seeds: Sow thinly, cover with 0.5–1 cm compost (check packet), mist.
  4. Water: Slowly until a little drains from the bottom. Don’t flood.
  5. Position: Brightest spot you have. Rotate weekly for even growth.
  6. Label: Date + variety. It helps more than you think.

Common issue: over-watering. If the top 2–3 cm feels damp, wait a day.

Weekly rhythm

  • Water when the top 2–3 cm is dry to the touch.
  • Feed lightly every 2 weeks once growth is steady (tomato feed for fruiting plants; general feed for leafy herbs).
  • Harvest little and often (salads: snip outer leaves, herbs: pinch above a leaf pair).
  • Remove tired leaves — compost them, and let new shoots take over.

Check in at the same time each week — make it a ritual with your tea or coffee. Growing is mindfulness in disguise.

Next steps

Once you’ve grown one pot, you’ll want to try another. Here are simple progressions:

  • Second pot: add a companion, like basil + tomato, or parsley + spring onion.
  • Windowsill rail: link two or three troughs together for salads and herbs.
  • Vertical step: hang pots or use a crate tower to multiply space.
  • Mindful moment: notice how tending your pots slows your breathing and lightens your day.
