Your First Pot

This guide will cover everything you need to begin: choosing a pot, ensuring good drainage, selecting compost or soil, and picking your first seeds.

Pick a Pot

Start with something about the size of a mixing bowl. Terracotta, plastic, or recycled containers with drainage holes all work.

Drainage Matters

A few small holes at the base will stop roots from drowning. A thin layer of grit or broken crockery can help too.

Soil or Compost

Multipurpose compost works well. Avoid heavy clay-based soil. A light, crumbly mix helps roots breathe and grow strong.

Starter Seeds

Lettuce, herbs (like basil or parsley), or radishes are excellent beginner choices—they germinate fast and are easy to care for.
