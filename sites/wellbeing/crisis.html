Crisis

If you feel unsafe, overwhelmed, or at risk of harming yourself or someone else, use the steps below. They’re practical, fast, and designed for when thinking clearly is hard. If immediate danger is present, contact emergency services in your area.

Your Traffic-Light Plan

Name your state; pick the matching action.

  • Green (OK): daily anchors, sleep window, meals, small social contact.
  • Amber (rising): use grounding; shorten plans; postpone big decisions.
  • Red (unsafe): follow your safety plan; remove means; call for help.
Build a 1-page safety plan

Fast Grounding (60–120s)

  1. Feet on floor. Notice the contact points.
  2. Name 5 things you see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste.
  3. Box breathing 4-4-4-4 for 4 cycles.

If thoughts spiral: say out loud, “I’m safe right now. This wave will pass.”

Reset Your Space

  • Open a window; change position (sit/stand/walk 30s).
  • Drink a full glass of water.
  • Lower harsh light; reduce noise or use calm music.

1-Page Safety Plan

Write it when calm; keep it visible.

  • Signals I’m not OK: (e.g., racing thoughts, can’t sleep, skipping meals)
  • What helps fast: (breathing, shower, walk, text a friend, music)
  • People I can contact: Names + numbers
  • Safe places: a room, a bench, a public space
  • Professional support: GP/therapist lines; local urgent numbers
Try a guided calm

Remove Means

Make the next harmful step harder to do.

  • Lock away or move items you could use to harm yourself.
  • Ask someone to temporarily hold medications or keys if needed.
  • Choose a “safe room” (no harmful items); go there when overwhelmed.

Call for Help

If you can’t stay safe, call your local emergency number or an urgent mental health line in your area.

  • Say: “I’m struggling to stay safe and need support now.”
  • Keep your address/postcode visible near the phone.

Texting or online chat can be easier if speech is hard — search your area’s crisis services.

Debrief (10 minutes)

  • What set it off? (trigger, tiredness, conflict, pain)
  • What helped? (tools, people, place)
  • One change for next time (note it on your safety card)

Rebuild Anchors

Simple, repeatable cues steady your system.

  • Morning: water + meds + 2-minute calm
  • Midday: eat something + 5-minute walk or stretch
  • Evening: wind-down window and a consistent bedtime

Let Someone Know

Message a trusted person: “Yesterday was rough. I’m safe today. Can we check in this week?”

Helpful Links

  • Search “crisis helpline” + your area for real-time support lines and webchat.
  • Your GP or local urgent mental health service can advise on next steps.

Web pages help with ideas, but emergencies need real-time human support.
