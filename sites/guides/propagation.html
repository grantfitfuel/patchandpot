Grafting

üçé Why graft?
  • Match vigour & size with **rootstocks** (e.g., apples on M26/M9 for small spaces).
  • Speed to fruit; preserve varieties; disease resistance.
üî™ Whip & tongue (late winter)
  • Use dormant scion & rootstock of pencil thickness.
  • Make matching sloping cuts with a tongue; bind & seal.
  • Heal in cool, frost-free place; plant when growth begins.
üå± Chip & T-budding (summer)
  • For roses/stone fruit; bark slips easily in warm weather.
  • Secure bud with budding tape; cut back stock above bud once it takes.

Cold/windy zones: choose hardy rootstocks; site against a warm wall. Protect unions from wind rock.

Layering

  • Simple layering: Peg a low shoot; nick underside; cover with gritty compost; keep moist (berries, climbers).
  • Air layering: For figs/camellias; girdle lightly, wrap in moist moss + film; pot once rooted.
  • Strawberries: Peg runners into pots; cut from mother when rooted.

Division

  • Best when dormant or right after flowering (avoid heat waves).
  • Sharp spade/knife; replant with compost & water in.
  • Great for chives, rhubarb (crowns), mint (in pots), many perennials.

  • Prick out at first true leaves into modules; firm lightly.

Tools & kit

  • Clean sharp knife/secateurs, labels, alcohol wipes.
  • Peat-free compost + perlite/sand; small pots/modules.
  • Clear lids/bags, elastic, pegs; rooting hormone (optional).
  • Heat mat/propagator (north & islands benefit most).
  • Fleece/cloches; windbreak mesh.
  • Tape & grafting compound (for grafts).
