Your First Pot

A simple, forgiving setup for salads, herbs, and easy veg — windowsill, doorstep, or balcony.

What you need

  • 25–30 cm pot with drainage
  • Peat-free compost
  • Seeds or a small plant (basil, parsley, salad)
  • Watering can / jug
  • Label + pencil

Light picker

LightGood first choices
Bright / 5–6h+Cherry tomatoes (bush), chillies, basil
Part sun / 3–4hParsley, mint (own pot), rocket, spring onions
Shaded / indirectMizuna, lettuce mixes, chives

Step-by-step

  1. Prep: Cover the hole with a shard/mesh so compost doesn’t leak.
  2. Fill: Compost to 2 cm below the rim (space for watering).
  3. Plant: Seedlings at same depth; or sow thinly and cover 0.5–1 cm.
  4. Water: Slowly until a little drains from the bottom.
  5. Place: Brightest spot; rotate weekly.
  6. Label: Variety + date.

Common issue: over-watering. If the top 2–3 cm is damp, wait a day.

Do

  • Stake early if growing a bush tomato.
  • Water in the morning, at the soil line.
  • Harvest little and often (outer leaves first).

Don’t

  • Pack plants until leaves touch (disease risk).
  • Mix very thirsty with drought lovers in one pot.
  • Forget labels — it helps more than you think.
