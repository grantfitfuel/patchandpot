Companion Planting (That Works in Pots)

Pair crops that genuinely help in containers: better pollination, fewer pests, stronger flavour — without cramming or chaos.

Core principles for containers

Match needs

  • Group by light (full sun vs part shade).
  • Group by thirst (don’t mix droughty rosemary with thirsty basil).
  • Group by feed (heavy feeders like tomatoes need richer mix).

Keep airflow

  • Pick a pot that fits the adult size.
  • Stake tall plants early so companions aren’t smothered.
  • Leaf contact = faster disease spread. Leave light between stems.

Rule of thumb: one “main crop” + one “helper” + (optionally) one “edge” plant. More than three in a 20–30 L pot gets messy.

Small-space layouts that just work

Tomato + Basil (20–30 L+)

  • 1 bush cherry tomato (centre, staked)
  • 2–3 basil around the rim
  • Optional: trailing thyme at edge

Shared sun & water needs; basil boosts pollinator traffic.

Chilli + Marigold (15–20 L)

  • 1–2 compact chillies
  • 2 French marigolds as underplanting

Colour draws pollinators; scent helps distract pests.

Kale + Chives (20 L)

  • 2 dwarf kales
  • 1 clump chives at the edge

Chives help deter aphids; both tolerate cooler, breezier spots.

Courgette + Nasturtium (40 L)

  • 1 courgette in centre (stake/space leaves)
  • 2 nasturtiums spilling over rim

Nasturtiums attract pollinators and may “hold” aphids.

Carrot + Spring Onion (deep trough)

  • Carrots on a loose grid
  • Spring onions dotted between rows

Onion scent can confuse carrot fly; keep the mix evenly moist.

Best pairings by goal

Pollination & yield

  • Tomato + basil, calendula
  • Courgette + nasturtium, borage (if space permits)
  • Strawberry + alyssum, thyme (edge)

Pest distraction

  • Brassicas + nasturtium (aphid magnet)
  • Chilli/pepper + marigold
  • Beans + dill (hoverflies love dill)

Flavour & growth

  • Tomato + basil (classic)
  • Cucumber + dill
  • Beetroot + coriander (edge, light shade)

Flowers & herbs for beneficial insects

Top draws for balconies

  • Calendula (long season, easy in pots)
  • Alyssum (edges, drought-tolerant)
  • Dill & fennel (umbels for hoverflies)
  • Marigolds (French types for compact growth)
  • Borage (only in big tubs; bee magnet)

How to use them

  • Dot small flowers between big-leaf crops, not against stems.
  • Keep them unsprayed; even “organic” sprays can hit pollinators.
  • Deadhead often = constant nectar.

Trap crops & living mulch (container-safe)

Trap crops

  • Nasturtium for aphids — place on the windward side so pests meet it first.
  • Radish as a fast sacrificial for flea beetle near brassicas.

Living mulch

  • Thyme or oregano as a dry edge under tomatoes/peppers (large pots only).
  • Alyssum to keep the surface cool in hot spells.

Mulches reduce splash and water loss — just don’t swamp the main crop’s base.

What to avoid (especially in pots)

  • Don’t mix plants with opposite watering needs (rosemary + basil).
  • Don’t underplant with thirsty mint (use mint only in its own pot).
  • Don’t pack leaves tight — airflow beats most disease.
  • Don’t let tall plants shade your sun-lovers all day.
