Support for Abuse Survivors

Abuse is never your fault. Whether physical, emotional, financial, or psychological, it can leave lasting scars. Recognising abuse and finding safe support are important steps towards safety and recovery.

Types of abuse

Physical abuse

Hitting, pushing, restraining, or using physical force to cause harm. Injuries may be visible, but fear and control are often the deeper impacts.

Emotional and psychological abuse

Insults, threats, humiliation, gaslighting, or isolation. These undermine self-worth and independence over time.

Coercive control

Patterns of behaviour that strip away freedom and autonomy. May include controlling finances, monitoring movements, or restricting friendships.

Financial abuse

Controlling access to money, sabotaging employment, or forcing financial dependence. Survivors may feel unable to leave due to money restrictions.

Online and digital abuse

Harassment, stalking, revenge porn, or technology used for control (e.g. monitoring devices, abusive messages).

Recognising the warning signs

  • You feel you must "walk on eggshells" to avoid conflict.
  • You’re criticised or put down constantly, even in front of others.
  • Your money, phone, or movements are restricted or monitored.
  • You’ve been isolated from friends, family, or work.
  • You feel unsafe in your own home or relationship.

Steps towards safety

If you’re not in immediate danger, small steps can build towards safety:

  • Confide in a trusted friend, colleague, or neighbour.
  • Prepare an emergency bag with essentials (documents, keys, medication).
  • Keep important numbers written down, not just stored on a device.
  • Reach out to helplines or local services listed below.

Where to find help

Scotland

  • Scottish Domestic Abuse & Forced Marriage Helpline – 24/7 confidential support
  • Scottish Women’s Aid – support, refuge, and advocacy
  • Men’s Advice Line – support for men experiencing abuse
  • SAMH – mental health support alongside abuse recovery

UK-wide

  • National Domestic Abuse Helpline – free, 24/7 support
  • Galop – LGBTQ+ anti-violence charity
  • Refuge – practical and emotional support
  • Victim Support – independent, free help for anyone affected
