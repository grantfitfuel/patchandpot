Crisis Help

If you’re at risk of harming yourself or someone else, help is available now. Pick the option that fits your situation.

If you are in immediate danger

Call emergency services now.

Call 999 (UK)

In the EU call 112. Elsewhere, use your local emergency number.

Can’t speak? Call 999, listen, then press 55 when prompted to use the Silent Solution (UK).

Go to A&E if you can get there safely, or ask someone to take you.

  • Take essentials (phone, ID, medication).
  • Tell the clinician: “I’m in crisis and need immediate support.”

If you’re not in immediate danger

Ground yourself (2–5 minutes)

  1. 4–6 Breathing: inhale 4, exhale 6.
  2. 5–4–3–2–1: name 5 things you see · 4 feel · 3 hear · 2 smell · 1 taste.
  3. Cold water splash on face/neck.
  4. Say what’s happening: “This is a surge. It will pass.”

Why this happens: Anxiety, Overwhelm & Depression

Your autonomic nervous system (ANS) regulates stress and calm. When life feels too heavy, these states can take over:

  • Anxiety / Fight–Flight — your sympathetic “gas pedal” is stuck on. You feel jittery, thoughts race, chest tightens. Everyday overwhelm often looks like this — too many tasks, deadlines, or responsibilities at once.
  • Overwhelm / Shutdown — when the system is overloaded, the parasympathetic “brake” slams down. You feel numb, detached, foggy. This is the freeze or collapse response: the brain saying “pause to survive.”
  • Depression — long periods of shutdown can slide into depression: loss of motivation, heavy body, lack of joy, hopeless thoughts. It’s the nervous system stuck in energy-conserving mode, even when danger is gone.

Understanding this helps: these states are not “failures,” but survival circuits firing. Practices like slow exhaling, safe connection, gentle movement, and thought reframing teach the system to come back toward balance.

Send “Struggling” or even an emoji 🚦
