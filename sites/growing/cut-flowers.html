Pots and planters: sizes and spacing

Use the guide below to choose a pot size and plant count. Drainage holes are non-negotiable. Add bricks or straps to anchor on balconies and railings.

  • Support: install mesh or canes at planting. Wind breaks stems, not just pots.
  • Watering: daily in heat; add saucers you can remove after a soak. Self watering inserts help.
  • Weight: add gravel or bricks in base for stability where wind is a risk.

Deep pots (30 to 40 cm) with a wide base are far more wind safe on balconies and rooftops.

Soil mixes and feeding

Standard cut flower mix

  • 60% peat-free compost
  • 30% fine bark or composted wood fibre
  • 10% grit or perlite for drainage
  • Slow release fertiliser for 10 to 12 weeks

Feeding plan

  • Weeks 0 to 4: just water, keep evenly moist.
  • Weeks 5+: add liquid feed weekly at half strength.
  • Heavy feeders (dahlia, sunflower, zinnia): feed twice weekly in hot spells.

Top up mix mid season in big tubs: scratch in 1 to 2 cm fresh compost and water in.

Where to grow: beyond beds

  • Balconies and roof terraces: anchor pots, use windbreak mesh, and stake early.
  • Railings and fences: hang boxes with metal brackets; cable tie to stop movement.
  • Window boxes: drought is the killer. Use a trough 20 cm deep and water daily in summer.
  • Steps and stoops: cluster 10 L tubs; rotate in fresh sowings for constant colour.
  • Shared courtyards: agree a corner as a cutting station; use troughs against sunniest wall.
  • Alleyways and path edges: narrow troughs with grid supports give lots of stems for tiny space.

Hardy annuals in pots (cool tolerant)

What to grow
  • Calendula, cornflower, nigella, ammi, scabious, dill, larkspur (cool frame).
  • Sow in modules Sep to Oct (mild areas) or Feb to Apr. Plant out with mesh support.
Container timing tips
  • Autumn sowing gives the earliest flowers where winters are milder and winds are managed.
  • In cooler or windy regions start in spring and harden off slowly with fleece on breezy days.

Half hardy annuals in pots (need frost free)

What to grow
  • Cosmos, snapdragon, rudbeckia, sunflower, amaranthus, zinnia (warmest sites), celosia (very warm).
  • Sow late March to April (north: April). Plant out late May to June (north: June).
  • Pinch for branching; stake early in the pot.

Cooler zones: choose dwarf or early series. Zinnia need the warmest, sunniest, most sheltered pots.

Bulbs, corms and tubers in containers

  • Spring: tulips (plant Nov for long stems), narcissus, alliums.
  • Summer: lilies (pots; stake), gladioli (stagger plantings), dahlias (one per 40 L tub).
  • Drainage is critical. Raise pots on feet; water deeply, then allow to drain.

Simple succession plan for pots

Early season

  • Feb to Apr sow: calendula, cornflower, nigella, dill in modules.
  • Plant out with mesh; start the first 10 L tubs.

Main season

  • Apr to Jun sow: cosmos, snapdragon, rudbeckia. Top up earlier tubs with a second wave.
  • Every 2 to 3 weeks add a fresh pot to keep stems coming.

Harvest and conditioning

  • Cut early morning. Clean buckets. Strip lower leaves.
  • Stage of cut: snapdragon at colour showing, cosmos with first petals open, zinnia half open.
  • Re cut under water. Use preservative or sugar + acid + tiny bleach. Rest cool for 2 to 4 hours.

UK regional notes

Scotland and windy coasts
  • Start half hardy plants later. Use windbreak mesh. Stake and tie at planting.
  • Choose early or compact series. Use fleece on breezy days while hardening off.
Milder south and city heat islands
  • Autumn sow hardy annuals for extra early flowers in pots.
  • Watch for mildew in heat. Water deeply and mulch the pot surface.
