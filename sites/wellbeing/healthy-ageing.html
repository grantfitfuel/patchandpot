Healthy Ageing

Ageing well is about systems that stack: joint-friendly movement, strength for everyday tasks, balance to prevent falls, food that supports body, brain, and microbiome, deep sleep, and connection. This guide explains the why, then gives clear, do-able steps with sets and reps and safe form cues.

1) Why strength, balance & brain health matter

  • Muscle & bone: from ~50, muscle and bone decline faster unless loaded. Strength work protects independence (stairs, shopping, rising from a chair).
  • Balance: prevents falls — a major cause of injury and fear of movement. Practising steadiness retrains ankles, hips, and core reactions.
  • Brain: movement increases blood flow and supports growth factors linked to memory and mood; sleep consolidates learning.
  • Microbiome: gut microbes influence inflammation, metabolism, and even brain signalling — diet diversity matters.

2) Sets vs reps (quick definitions)

Reps (repetitions) = how many times you do an exercise in a row. Sets = how many rounds of those reps you complete, with rests between sets.

Example: “2 sets × 10 reps” means: do 10 reps, rest ~45s–60s, do 10 more.

3) Stretching & mobility (move better, hurt less)

Daily 6–8 minute warm-up

  • Neck circles (small), shoulder rolls — 1 set × 8 reps each
  • Arm swings (gentle), thoracic rotations — 1 × 8
  • Hip circles, ankle circles — 1 × 10 each side
  • Calf stretch at wall — 2 × 20s per side

Mobility tips

  • Exhale longer than inhale (e.g., in 4 / out 6) to reduce muscle guarding.
  • Warmth helps (shower or heat pack on tight spots) before moving.
  • Seek a “comfortable stretch,” not pain; increase range gradually.

Common tight spots

  • Calves/ankles (affect balance & walking push-off)
  • Hips/glutes (stairs, getting up)
  • Thoracic spine (posture, shoulder reach)

4) Strength training (bones, joints, independence)

Starter circuit (10–15 minutes)

1) Sit-to-Stand

Sets: 2–3   Reps: 8–12   Tempo: 2s down, 1s up

From a chair, feet hip-width, lean slightly forward, stand tall; sit down slowly under control. Progress by pausing 2s at the bottom or holding light weights.

2) Wall Push-ups

Sets: 2–3   Reps: 8–12

Hands on wall at chest height, body long. Lower with elbows at ~45°; press away. Progress to kitchen-counter push-ups.

3) Hip Hinge

Sets: 2–3   Reps: 8–12

Hands on thighs; push hips back with a neutral spine; slight knee bend; stand tall and squeeze glutes. Progress by holding a light weight or backpack.

4) March & Balance

Sets: 2   Reps: 30–45s marching

March on the spot. Hold a chair if needed. Progress to single-leg stands 10–20s per side.

Rest 30–60s between sets. If you have long-term conditions, check with your clinician first.

Bone health

Include weight-bearing moves (standing, marching, step-ups). If appropriate, add light impact (gentle heel drops 2 × 10).

Arthritis tips

Short, frequent sessions beat long ones. Warm up well; keep loads light/moderate; finish with a mobility cool-down.

Progression

When 12 reps feel easy with good form, add a little weight (0.5–1 kg) or an extra set. Keep movements smooth, no jerks.

5) Walking (foundation for heart, brain & mood)

  • Cadence: comfortable conversation pace; add 1–3 minute brisk bursts (recover as needed).
  • Posture: tall through crown, relaxed shoulders, light arm swing.
  • Feet: roll through the foot; occasional grass or gravel (if safe) challenges balance.
  • Brain: nature & social walking reduce stress and improve adherence.

6) Swimming & water exercise (low-impact, whole-body)

Why it helps

  • Buoyancy reduces joint load; water provides even resistance for strength & endurance.
  • Cooler environment helps temperature control; rhythm calms the nervous system.

Getting started

  • Begin with 15–20 minutes; add 2–5 minutes weekly.
  • Aqua-aerobics = structure + social motivation.
  • Use noodles/floats for confidence and technique practice.

Safety

  • Warm shoulders/hips before entering; exit slowly to avoid dizziness.
  • Heart/balance conditions? Check clinician advice.

7) Balance & steadiness (Tai Chi • Qi Gong • Yoga • Chair Yoga)

Tai Chi

Slow, flowing sequences that train weight shift, ankle strategy, and focused breathing. Strong evidence for fall reduction.

Qi Gong

Gentle, repetitive patterns + breath. Excellent for joint-friendly mobility and calm attention.

Yoga (gentle)

Choose beginner/chair classes. Focus on hip hinges, spinal length, and breath-led movement. Osteoporosis: avoid deep spinal flexion/rotation.

Chair Yoga

All poses adapted seated/standing with support. Builds confidence for limited mobility or balance concerns.

At-home balance mini-set

  • Feet together → semi-tandem → full tandem — Sets: 2   Hold: 20–30s
  • Single-leg stand — Sets: 2   Hold: 10–20s per side (fingertip support)
  • Heel-toe walking along a line — Sets: 2 hallway lengths

8) Neurodiversity & sensory needs (make it workable)

Executive function support

  • Use If–Then anchors: “If kettle boils → Then 2-minute mobility.”
  • Visual prompts: post-it on the remote/door; checklist on the fridge.
  • Short “wins”: 3–5 minute micro-sessions sprinkled through the day.

Sensory profiles

  • Over-sensitive? Reduce noise (earbuds), light (cap), and choose predictable routes/pools.
  • Under-sensitive? Add rhythm/beat, brisker intervals, textured surfaces (safe) for feedback.

Clear instructions

  • One cue at a time: “Stand tall. Hips back. Pause.”
  • Use timers (2–10 min). End on success to encourage repetition.
  • Routine > variety: repeat the same simple circuit on set days.

9) Nutrition & the microbiome (feed your helpers)

Daily “plant points”

Aim for variety: 2–3 veg + 1 fruit per main meal; beans/lentils most days; whole grains; nuts/seeds.

Fibre targets

Build toward ~25–30g/day (gradually, with fluids). Oats, beans, berries, veg, whole grains.

Fermented foods

Live yoghurt/kefir, sauerkraut/kimchi, kombucha (if tolerated). Start small if new to you.

Inflammation & joints

Omega-3s (oily fish or algae), spices (turmeric/ginger), colourful plants; limit ultra-processed foods.

Digestive conditions? Adjust fibre/ferments with your clinician or dietitian.

10) Macronutrients: how to build plates

Protein (muscle & repair)

Include at each meal (eggs, fish, poultry, tofu/tempeh, beans, Greek yoghurt, lentils). Many older adults do well with ~20–30g protein per meal split across the day.

Carbohydrates (energy)

Prefer slow-release: oats, wholegrain bread, brown rice, quinoa, beans, root veg. Pair with protein & fats for steady energy.

Fats (hormones & brain)

Olive oil, nuts, seeds, avocado, oily fish or algae. Limit trans/industrial fats.

11) Blueberries & brain foods (polyphenols for cognition)

  • Berries: polyphenols support blood flow & antioxidant defences.
  • Leafy greens: folate & carotenoids for brain & eye health.
  • Nuts & seeds: vitamin E, magnesium, healthy fats.
  • Tea/coffee: can aid focus; avoid late if sleep suffers.

12) Hydration (small sips, big difference)

  • Keep water visible; flavour with lemon, mint, berries if it helps.
  • Use a small glass and refill often if a big bottle feels daunting.
  • Soups, stews, fruit/veg contribute to fluids; mind evening intake if night-time toilet trips disrupt sleep.

13) Cognition & sleep (repair and remember)

Sleep basics

  • Regular bed/wake times; dim lights 1–2h before bed.
  • Warm bath/shower; if awake >20 min, move to a calm room until sleepy.

See Sleep & Insomnia for full toolkits.

Brain training

  • Learn new skills (language, music, crafts) and keep socially engaged.
  • Combine movement + thinking (dance steps, garden planning, map reading on walks).

Hearing & vision

Untreated hearing/vision loss increases cognitive load. Get checked; use aids consistently.

14) Starter programmes (Weeks 1–4)

A) Limited mobility / confidence-building

  • Strength — Sit-to-Stand, Wall Push-ups, Hip Hinge: 2 sets × 8–10 reps, 2×/week
  • Balance — Tandem stands: 2 sets × 20s; March in place: 2 × 30–45s, 3×/week
  • Walking — 5–10 min most days at easy pace

Week 3–4: add a 3rd set or light weights.

B) General foundation (no major limitations)

  • Strength — Circuit above: 3 sets × 8–12 reps, 2–3×/week
  • Balance — Mini-set plus heel-toe walking: 2 hallway lengths, 3×/week
  • Walking — 20–30 min most days, with 2× 2-min brisk bursts
  • Mobility — 6–8 min warm-up daily

C) Water-friendly focus

  • Pool — 15–25 min swim or aqua-aerobics, 2–3×/week
  • Strength — Chair-based circuit: 2–3 sets × 8–12 reps, 2×/week
  • Balance — Single-leg stands: 2 × 10–20s per side, most days
