Neurolinguistics

Words point your brain. Small swaps reduce friction and make action more likely.

Swap 1: “Have to” → “Choose to (2 min)”

“I choose to water two plants for 2 minutes.” Choice + small timebox = lower resistance.

Swap 2: “I’m bad at” → “I’m practising”

Practise allows wobble and improvement. “I’m practising potting mix today.”

Swap 3: Always/Never → Specific

“Last time I forgot; this time I’ll put the seeds by the kettle.”

If–Then Plans

  • If kettle on → water one pot.
  • If text arrives → stand up to read it.

Friendly Self-Talk

“This feels hard. I’ll do 2 minutes and see.”

Boundary Scripts

“I can do X today; I can’t do Y. Let’s plan Y tomorrow.”
