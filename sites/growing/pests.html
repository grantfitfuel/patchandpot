Common Pests — Quick ID & Fixes

Spot what’s wrong in seconds. Try a gentle fix today. Keep predators around so you rarely need sprays.

Prevention that actually works in pots

  • Airflow: don’t cram; stake early so leaves don’t touch.
  • Water roots, not leaves: morning so plants dry fast.
  • Cleanliness: remove yellowing leaves; wipe pot rims (snails hide here).
  • Companion flowers: dill, calendula, alyssum for hoverflies/ladybirds.
  • Quarantine newbies: keep new plants separate for a week.
  • Feed steady, not heavy: lush, sappy growth invites pests.

Most outbreaks start when plants are thirsty and cramped. Fix those, half the battle’s won.

Quick ID (symptom → likely pest)

SymptomLikely culpritSeverity
Sticky leaves, curled tips, antsAphidsMedium
Fine webbing, speckled leaves (dry air)Spider mitesMedium
Big ragged holes + slime trailsSlugs & snailsHigh
White cloud when you touch plantWhiteflyMedium
Snaking silver tunnels in leavesLeaf minerLow
Tomato leaves blotchy, stems blacken in wetTomato blightHigh

Severity is about speed & spread in containers. Slugs/snails can erase a pot overnight; blight races in wet spells.

Rule: If 20%+ of a small plant is hit, act today. For big plants, remove worst leaves and monitor for 48 hours.

Aphids Medium

StickyCurled tipsAnts nearby
  • Blast with water (undersides too). Repeat in 2–3 days.
  • Wipe with mild soapy water; rinse after 10–15 mins.
  • Plant dill/alyssum; don’t spray predators away.

Spider mites Medium

Fine webSpeckled leavesDry air
  • Mist daily to raise humidity; shower plant gently.
  • Wipe both sides of leaves; improve airflow.
  • Move away from radiators/sun-baked glass.

Slugs & Snails High

Big holesSilvery trails
  • Evening hand-pick; check under rims/saucers.
  • Copper tape on pot sides; wool pellets as barrier.
  • Raise pots on feet; reduce damp hiding spots.

Whitefly Medium

White cloudSticky leaves
  • Yellow sticky cards just above foliage.
  • Wipe with soapy water; rinse. Improve airflow.
  • Avoid overfeeding soft growth.

Leaf miner Low

Snake trailsThin tunnels
  • Remove affected leaves quickly (bin them).
  • Use fine mesh on susceptible crops.

Tomato blight High

Brown patchesBlackened stemsWet spells
  • Remove affected leaves/fruit; don’t compost.
  • Keep foliage dry: water soil only; increase spacing.
  • Prioritise fast ripening; consider cover in wet weeks.

Vine weevil (pots) Medium

Notched leavesSudden wilt
  • Check roots for grubs when repotting; bin infested compost.
  • Use physical barriers on pot rims; tidy hiding spots.

When to act (and with what)

SituationAction today
Seedlings disappearing overnightSlug patrol at dusk; copper on pots; raise trays
10–20% leaf area with aphidsWater blast + soapy wipe; add dill/alyssum
Mites on indoor windowsillMist daily + shower leaves; move off radiator
Blight in a wet weekStrip worst leaves; water at soil; increase spacing

Your non-spray toolkit

  • Hand sprayer + mild soap
  • Yellow sticky cards (whitefly)
  • Copper tape / pot feet / wool pellets
  • Fine mesh/fleece for short “danger windows”

Most chemical “cures” also knock out the predators you need. Go gentle first.

Regional & wind notes (quick)

  • Windy coasts / rooftops: stake early, use windbreak mesh, cluster pots to share load.
  • Mild south & city heat islands: watch whitefly and mites; airflow + steady watering.
  • Cooler, wetter regions: space tomatoes wider; keep foliage dry to dodge blight.
