Therapies & Tools

What they are, why they help, and how to try them safely. Everything below is written to be practical and kind, with links to one-page printables you can keep beside you.

1) CBT (Cognitive Behavioural Therapy)

What it is

Structured skills to spot unhelpful thought patterns, experiment with new behaviours, and reduce symptoms over time.

Try this

  • Keep a brief thought–feeling–action note once a day.
  • Pick one tiny behaviour change and test it this week.

2) Neuroscience & brain change

Key ideas

  • Practice wires together; sleep consolidates; stress narrows options.
  • Gentle movement and breath work downshift alarm systems.

Try this

  • 90-second reset: slow inhale, longer exhale × 8–10 breaths.
  • Tiny habit: attach a 2-minute tool to a daily anchor (kettle, teeth, door).

3) Neurolinguistics & reframing

Patterns

  • All-or-nothing, fortune-telling, mind-reading, catastrophising.

Try this

  • Swap “I always fail” → “I’ve struggled before, and I’m learning.”
  • Ask, “What would I say to a friend?”

How to use

  • Pair with breath or movement for better stickiness.

6) Breathing & grounding

Try this

  • 4–6 breathing: inhale 4, exhale 6–8, for ~2 minutes.
  • 5-senses grounding: 5 see, 4 touch, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste.

Grounding techniques

  • Temperature reset: cool water on wrists or a wrapped cold pack on the neck for 30–60s.
  • Object focus: hold a textured item; describe weight, temperature, edges, colour.
  • Name & locate: “I am [name], in [room], it’s [day/time]. I can feel my feet on the floor.”

7) Window of Tolerance & Polyvagal Reset

What it is

  • Window of Tolerance: the zone where stress is manageable and thinking is flexible.
  • Polyvagal modes: fight/flight (high), freeze/shut-down (low), and social safety (balanced).

Try this

  • Check-in: “Too high? Too low? Or in my window?”
  • Reset: long exhale, humming, grounding touch, connect with someone safe.

8) Behavioural Activation (BA)

What it is

First-line treatment for depression in NICE guidelines. We plan tiny, meaningful actions and track how they affect mood.

Try this

  • Choose 3 daily actions: Body (walk, stretch), Connection (call/text), Meaning (plant, read, tidy one shelf).
  • Do them even if motivation is low; keep them small and repeatable.

9) Values & Acceptance (ACT)

What it is

Acceptance & Commitment Therapy builds a life around your values. We make space for difficult thoughts and still move toward what matters.

Try this

  • List your top 3 values (e.g., family, creativity, kindness).
  • Ask: “What tiny step today would honour one of these?”
  • If/then plan: “If anxiety shows up, then I’ll take one small valued step.”

10) Self-Compassion Practices

What it is

Grounded in research (Kristin Neff and others). Self-compassion reduces anxiety and depression and increases resilience.

Try this

  • Hand on chest; breathe slowly; say: “This is hard. May I be kind to myself.”
  • Write a brief letter to yourself from a caring friend’s perspective.

11) Journaling prompts

Prompts

  • “What helped a tiny bit today?”
  • “What would future-me thank me for doing next?”
  • “What’s one thing I can leave for tomorrow?”
