Carer Support

Caring for someone you love is an act of devotion — but it can also feel like carrying the weight of the world. This page explores why caring is so emotionally demanding, how it links with stress, anxiety and depression, and what you can do to protect your own wellbeing.

1. Why Caring Feels Overwhelming

Carers often carry an invisible load: constant responsibility, emotional strain, disrupted sleep, financial pressure, and the grief of watching someone decline. This mix can lead to:

  • Anxiety and overwhelm: Feeling constantly “on alert”, struggling to switch off or rest.
  • Low mood or depression: The sense that nothing will improve, or that you’re losing yourself in the role.
  • Guilt: Believing you should do more, even when you’re already at breaking point.
  • Isolation: Friendships and hobbies shrinking because of time and energy demands.

2. Anticipatory Grief and After Loss

Carers often begin grieving long before death — a loved one with dementia may no longer recognise them, or a parent with terminal illness may no longer be the person they once knew. This is called anticipatory grief, and it can bring sadness, anger, and exhaustion.

After loss, carers may feel “unmoored” — suddenly without the daily role that defined their days. It is common to feel relief and grief at the same time. Both are normal, human responses.

3. Practical Ways to Protect Your Wellbeing

Boundaries
Say “yes” to what matters, “no” where possible. One small limit (like not answering non-urgent calls at night) protects your energy.
Micro-breaks
Even 2 minutes with a cup of tea outside helps reset your nervous system. These “small resets” are as important as long rests.
Talk openly
Share with other carers — whether in a group, online forum, or trusted friend. Naming the weight makes it lighter.
Mind–body tools
Slow breathing, a short walk, or mindful gardening help calm the fight–flight system and reduce overwhelm.

4. Where to Find Support

You don’t have to carry this alone. These organisations can help:

  • Carers UK — national advice, helpline, and local groups.
  • Mind — mental health support and coping strategies.
  • Breathing Space (Scotland) — confidential listening service.
  • Scottish Association for Mental Health (SAMH) — help for carers and families.
  • NHS – Carer Support — practical advice and entitlements.
  • Carers Trust — local centres and peer support networks.

If you ever feel you are at breaking point, call Samaritans (116 123) or text SHOUT (text 85258).
