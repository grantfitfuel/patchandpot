Neuroscience

Why we do what we do — and how tiny, repeatable shifts change the brain. This page translates core neuroscience into tools you can use today: calmer emotion, steadier attention, better memory, less bias, more creativity, and gentler change.

Predictive Brain

Your brain is a prediction engine. It guesses what’s next, compares to senses, then updates. We notice mismatches as surprise, friction, or curiosity.

  • Make next steps obvious: put tools where the action happens.
  • Reduce surprises: routines, checklists, labels.
  • Use gentle novelty to spark focus (new mug, new playlist).

Nervous System States

We shift among calm (social/connected), mobilised (fight/flight), and shut down (freeze). State drives what feels possible.

  • Up-regulate: light + brisk walk + upbeat track.
  • Downshift: slow breath (4–6/min), shoulders down, sip water.
  • Repair: micro-rests between tasks.

Window of Tolerance

Tasks land better when inside your window. Too hyped or too flat? Adjust state first, then begin tiny.

Try the 2-minute reset

Emotion ≠ Instruction

  • Name it: “This is frustration.” Naming reduces limbic alarm.
  • Body first: exhale longer, jaw unclench, drop shoulders.
  • Micro-do: 2-minute step consistent with values.

Social Safety

Warm signals (tone, nods, kindness to self) quiet alarms and reopen thinking.

  • Soften face; slow your speech; hand on chest + breath.
  • Say: “This is hard and I can take one small step.”

2-Minute Reset

  • 4–4–6 breath × 8 cycles (exhale longer).
  • Step to a window; look far; sip water.
  • 60-sec neck/shoulder roll; stand and sway.

10-Minute Focus Box

  • One task; one tab; timer 10.
  • Stop while it’s easy. Leave a breadcrumb (next tiny step) for later.

Protect Focus

  • Batch pings; check messages on schedule.
  • Noise control (headphones, brown noise).
  • Visual control (hide piles, full-screen).

Body-Doubling

Work alongside someone (in person or on a quiet call) to reduce friction and finish the first inch.

Do the first pot together

Encode → Store → Retrieve

  • Encode: speak it; write one line; teach someone.
  • Store: sleep + spaced repeats (1d, 3d, 7d).
  • Retrieve: quiz yourself; don’t just re-read.

Context Cues

Place the thing where the action happens: seeds by kettle; gloves by the door.

Learning by Doing

Hands-on changes the brain faster. Plant, label, take one photo, write one line.

Design the View

  • Make the next step pop (contrast labels, clear boxes).
  • Hide competing inputs (covers, bins with lids).

Language Aims the Brain

  • “I choose to … for 2 minutes.”
  • “I’m practising …”
  • Specific beats “always/never”.
Micro-swaps

If–Then Scripts

If kettle on → water one pot. If text arrives → stand up to read it.

Common Traps

  • All-or-nothing; catastrophising
  • Mind-reading; fortune-telling
  • Confirmation bias; present bias

Counter-Moves

  • Write evidence for/against.
  • 80% done > stalled perfect.
  • Tiny test today; review later.
Reframing steps

Externalise

Use checklists and a buddy review to catch blind spots.

Diverge → Converge

  • Diverge: 10 scrappy ideas in 5 minutes.
  • Converge: pick 1 to test today.
  • Review after sleep or walk.

Incubation

Step away; do a light task. Ideas surface when the guard relaxes.

Helpful Constraints

Set time, budget, or materials limits to spark inventive solutions.

Find the Middle

Too little arousal → sluggish. Too much → scrambled. Adjust state to ride the middle.

Complete the Cycle

  • Move your body (2–10 minutes).
  • Deep social sigh (long exhale with someone safe).
  • Creative off-ramp: write, hum, doodle.

Boundaries Lower Load

Clarity reduces background threat. One yes → one no to protect capacity.

Habit Loops

  • Cue you always do (kettle).
  • Tiny action (water 1 pot).
  • Reward now (tick, sip, photo).

Friction Design

  • Reduce friction for desired acts (tools ready).
  • Increase friction for unhelpful ones (apps in a folder, sign-out).

Forgiving Streaks

Allow 2 skips per week. Restart without drama. Consistency over intensity.

Build your ladder

Dopamine (Drive)

  • Effort + clear reward (tick, photo log).
  • Novelty in tiny doses (new spot, new herb).
  • Avoid constant big spikes (scroll binges).

Serotonin (Steady)

  • Morning sunlight; walks.
  • Protein + complex carbs; colour on plate.
  • Connection + kindness to self.

Noradrenaline & GABA

  • Alerting: cold splash, light, posture.
  • Calming: slow exhale, lower shoulders, warm tea.

Sleep Architecture

90-min cycles; deep + REM. Learning and mood stabilise during sleep.

When Sleep Is Short

  • Light + movement on waking; hydrate.
  • Power nap 20–30 min (not late evening).
  • Extra checks on high-risk tasks.

Caffeine Timing

Delay ~60–90 minutes after waking. Avoid late-day doses that fragment sleep.

Balanced Plate

  • Protein at each meal stabilises energy.
  • Complex carbs + fibre for focus and mood.
  • Healthy fats; hydrate early and often.

Steady Energy

Pair carbs with protein; avoid long fasts if you crash; pack a small snack.

Alcohol & Sleep

Reduces REM and fragments night sleep; keep lighter on hard weeks.

Sensory Dials

  • Sound: headphones, brown noise.
  • Light: warm lamp, cap/hood, screen tint.
  • Touch: seam-free, soft layer, fidget.

Meltdown / Shutdown

Early cues: rising noise sensitivity, irritability, fuzziness. Step out; downshift; safe space; low demands.

Reasonable Adjustments

Noise control, breaks, written info, camera-off option, simple briefs, clear deadlines.

Green Day

  • One 25–50 min deep block; one admin batch.
  • Micro-novelty for dopamine (new spot, track).
  • Train a tiny habit you want tomorrow.

Amber Day

  • Focus box 10; stop early; breadcrumb next step.
  • Body-double; noise control; hide piles.
  • One win → celebrate; then low bar tasks.

Red Day

  • State first: breath, water, light, sway.
  • Bare minimum only (2-minute actions).
  • Protect recovery window; ask for help.
