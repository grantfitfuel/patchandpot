Propagation: Cuttings, Division & Grafting

Multiply plants for free — simple, clean methods that fit balconies, windowsills and tiny worktops.

Softwood Cuttings (spring–early summer)

Best for herbs (mint, rosemary, thyme, sage, oregano), fuchsias, pelargoniums.

  1. Snip 8–10 cm non-flowering shoot below a node; strip lower leaves.
  2. Dip base in water (optional rooting powder); insert into 50:50 compost:perlite.
  3. Firm gently; water; cover with a clear dome/bag off the leaves.
  4. Bright light, no direct midday sun; keep just moist. Root in 2–4 weeks.

Use a pencil or chopstick to make a pilot hole — avoids bruising the stem.

Semi-ripe & Hardwood Cuttings

For woody herbs and shrubs later in the season.

  1. Semi-ripe (late summer): 8–12 cm tip cuttings, strip lower leaves, pot as above; bottom warmth helps.
  2. Hardwood (late autumn–winter): pencil-thick sticks 15–20 cm, leafless; insert two nodes deep in gritty compost.
  3. Keep barely moist; shelter from severe frost; root over winter–spring.

Don’t overwater: most failures are from soggy compost and low oxygen at the base.

Division

Fast way to multiply clump-forming plants: chives, mint, oregano, thyme, strawberries (runners).

  1. Water the plant well the day before.
  2. Tip out; tease or slice into sections (each with roots + shoots).
  3. Repot divisions into fresh mix; trim top growth lightly; water in.

For strawberries, pot up runners while attached; cut the umbilical when rooted.

Layering (zero-drama)

Great for mint, thyme, rosemary, trailing strawberries.

  1. Pin a low stem to compost in a neighbour pot with a bent paperclip.
  2. Keep the pin spot moist; roots form at the node in 2–4 weeks.
  3. Cut free and pot on.

Water Propagation (windowsill quick wins)

For basil, mint, oregano, coleus, pothos.

  1. Cut a 10 cm tip under a node; strip lower leaves.
  2. Place in a jar; change water every 2–3 days.
  3. Pot into compost once roots are 3–5 cm long.

Grafting (edibles, optional)

Advanced but doable on a bench with a sharp blade. Common for tomatoes, cucurbits.

  1. Raise rootstock & scion to match stem thickness (3–4 mm).
  2. Cut both at 45°; align cambium; join with a silicone clip.
  3. High humidity & shade 5–7 days; then wean to normal light.

Why graft tomatoes? Rootstock resists disease, boosts vigour in cool container summers.
